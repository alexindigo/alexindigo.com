ac•cor•di•on |ə'kôrdēən|
noun
a portable musical instrument with metal reeds blown by bellows, played by means of keys and buttons: accordion [ as modifier ] : an accordion player. • [ as modifier ] folding like the bellows of an accordion: an accordion pleat. ORIGIN mid 19th cent.: from German Akkordion, from Italian accordare 'to tune'.
